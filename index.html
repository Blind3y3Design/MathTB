Summarize

The relationship between the squares of the sides of a right triangle is one of the most important theorems in mathematics. It is commonly known as the Pythagorean theorem, named after the ancient Greek mathematician Pythagoras, although it was known long before he noticed the relationship.

 
  • A Very Simple Proof
Save Submit

In a right triangle, the hypotenuse is the side opposite the right angle and is always the longest of the three sides.

The legs of the right triangle are the two sides that are not the hypotenuse. The legs always meet at the right angle.

In triangle ABC shown below, side c is the hypotenuse, and sides a and b are the legs.

Generally, for any right triangle:

Leg12+Leg22=Hypotenuse2

The legs may have different lengths, though, so each leg is assigned its own variable.

The Pythagorean theorem states that, in a right triangle, the sum of the area of the squares on the legs of the triangle is equal to the area of the square on the hypotenuse.

Stated algebraically, the Pythagorean theorem is often written as a2+b2=c2 for a right triangle with legs a and b and hypotenuse c.

Solving Problems with the Pythagorean Theorem

If you know the lengths of any two sides of a right triangle, you can find the length of the third side.

 
  • Find the Length of the Hypotenuse
Save Submit

Example 1: Find the Length of the Hypotenuse

Stacy is walking through the park and can choose to take the sidewalk or the bike path. How long is the bike path? Then, determine the difference between her two path choices.

Solution:

Since the three pieces form a right triangle, use the Pythagorean theorem.

a2+b2+c2

One leg of the triangle has a length of 72 meters, and the other leg has a length of 96 meters. The distance of the bike path is the unknown length. The variable c will be used to represent the unknown length, since it is the hypotenuse of a right triangle.

Therefore, the length of the bike path is 120 feet.

Taking the sidewalk, Stacy would have to walk 72 + 96 = 168 feet. Taking the bike path, however, she would only have to walk 120 feet.  The difference between the two paths is 168 – 120 = 48 feet.

Example 2: Find A Missing Leg

A 25' long cable is attached to a tower at a point 20' above the bridge deck. The other end of the cable will be attached to the deck.

 
  • Tower Point
Save Submit

How many feet from the base of the tower will the cable be attached?

Solution:

Use the Pythagorean theorem.

a2+b2=c2

One leg of the triangle has a length of 20 feet, and the other known side is the hypotenuse with a length of 25 feet. The distance from the base of the tower to the cable is an unknown leg of a right triangle, so the variable b can be used to represent its length.

The distance from the base of the tower to the cable is 15 feet long.
